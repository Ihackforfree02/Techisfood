Dragon Fruit Delight

Dragon fruit, or pitaya, is a tropical fruit from cactus plants known for its vibrant pink or yellow skin and speckled, juicy flesh. Its striking look, unique taste, and health benefits make it a favorite worldwide.

Native to Central and South America, dragon fruit is now grown in Southeast Asia, Israel, Australia, and the U.S. Its flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators like bats and moths.

Fun fact: the dragon fruit flower blooms only at night and releases a sweet fragrance to lure pollinators.

Farm to fork: After harvest, dragon fruits are carefully packed and transported to stores or markets worldwide, ensuring they arrive fresh and ready to enjoy.

Nutritional Value

Dragon fruit is low in calories but nutrient-rich. A 100g serving contains roughly 60 calories, 3g of fiber, vitamin C, magnesium, iron, and antioxidants like betalains and flavonoids that help protect your cells and support digestion.

Regular consumption may boost immunity, improve skin health, and maintain hydration thanks to its high water content.

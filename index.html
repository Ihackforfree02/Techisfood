About Dragon Fruit

Dragon fruit, also known as pitaya, is a tropical fruit native to Central America but widely cultivated in Southeast Asia. Its striking appearance, vibrant pink skin, and speckled flesh make it instantly recognizable. Known for its mildly sweet taste and crunchy seeds, dragon fruit is also packed with nutrients and antioxidants.

Origin

Originally from Mexico and Central America, dragon fruit is now grown in Thailand, Vietnam, Israel, and more.

Cultivation

Grows on a climbing cactus in tropical climates, often yielding multiple harvests per year.

Varieties

Common varieties include white-fleshed, red-fleshed, and yellow-skinned dragon fruit, each with subtle taste differences.

Health Benefits

Dragon fruit is rich in vitamin C, boosting the immune system, and vitamin B, which supports energy metabolism.

Contains calcium, iron, magnesium, and phosphorus, which promote bone health and overall body function.

Helps improve digestion, supports a healthy gut, and is full of antioxidants that protect cells from damage.

From Farm to Table

Planting

Dragon fruit is grown from cuttings and planted in fertile tropical soil.

Flowering

Night-blooming flowers attract pollinators and prepare for fruit production.

Harvest

After 30-50 days, fruits ripen and are hand-picked to ensure quality.

Distribution

Fruits are packaged and sent to markets locally and internationally.

Consumption

Enjoyed fresh, in smoothies, desserts, or exotic salads!

Tasty Recipes

Dragon Fruit Smoothie

Blend fresh dragon fruit, banana, coconut milk, and a hint of honey for a refreshing tropical drink.

Exotic Fruit Salad

Combine dragon fruit, kiwi, pineapple, and berries, topped with a squeeze of lime juice.

Frozen Pops

Freeze pureed dragon fruit with a touch of coconut water for a vibrant and healthy treat.

What is Dragon Fruit?

Dragon fruit, also called pitaya, comes from cactus plants originally found in Central America. Today it is grown in tropical and subtropical regions like Vietnam, Thailand, Israel, and warm parts of the US. Its skin is bright pink or yellow with green scales, while its inside flesh can be white, pink, or red, filled with tiny black seeds.

Dragon fruit flowers bloom at night and are pollinated by bats or moths. It tastes mildly sweet, with a texture similar to kiwi because of the crunchy seeds.

Fun Fact: One dragon fruit can contain more than a million seeds! It is a low-calorie fruit that’s hydrating and packed with antioxidants.

Life Cycle

  1. Seed or Cutting: Dragon fruit can start from seeds or cuttings planted on trellises for support.
  2. Vegetative Growth: Stems grow and branch out. Plants need sun, water, and warmth.
  3. Flowering: Large flowers bloom at night; pollination occurs naturally or manually.
  4. Fruit Set: Small fruits grow and mature within 30–50 days.
  5. Harvest: Ripe fruits are hand-picked carefully to avoid bruising.
  6. Packaging: Sorted, cleaned, and packed in crates for transport.
  7. Market: Sold fresh locally or exported for international consumption.

Farm to Fork Journey

Planting

Seeds or cuttings planted on farms in warm regions.

Growth

Cactus stems grow and start forming flowers under the sun.

Harvest

Hand-picked ripe fruits are sorted and packed carefully.

Transport

Packed in crates, shipped to markets and stores.

Consumption

Sold fresh and ready to enjoy in meals, smoothies, or desserts.

Recipes

Dragon Fruit Smoothie

Servings: 2

Ingredients: 1 dragon fruit, 1 banana, 200ml almond milk, honey to taste

Method: Blend all ingredients until smooth. Serve chilled.

Cooking Time: 5 mins

Dragon Fruit Salad

Servings: 3

Ingredients: 1 dragon fruit, 1 kiwi, 1 cup strawberries, mint leaves, lemon juice

Method: Chop all fruits, mix with lemon juice and mint leaves, serve fresh.

Cooking Time: 10 mins

Dragon Fruit Sorbet

Servings: 4

Ingredients: 2 dragon fruits, 1/4 cup sugar, 1/2 cup water, juice of 1 lime

Method: Puree dragon fruit, dissolve sugar in water, mix with lime juice and freeze for 3-4 hours.

Cooking Time: 15 mins prep + freezing

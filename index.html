What Dragon Fruit Is

Dragon fruit (pitaya/pitahaya) refers to fruits from several cactus species, notably Hylocereus and Selenicereus. The fruit is known for leathery, scale-like skin and flesh that may be white, pink, or deep red with tiny black seeds. Varieties differ in sweetness and color.

  • Origin: Native to Central & South America, naturalised in Southeast Asia.
  • Appearance: Bright skin with leafy scales — eye-catching on plates and markets.
  • Uses: Fresh eating, juices, smoothies, frozen desserts, and garnishes.

Nutritional Value

Dragon fruit is low-calorie, rich in fiber and vitamin C, and contains minerals such as magnesium. It provides antioxidants (betalains in red types) and small amounts of protein and healthy fats in seeds. Below are typical values per 100 g (approximate).

Calories

~57 kcal / 100 g

Fiber

~3 g / 100 g — helps digestion

Vitamin C

Supports immunity and skin health

Why include it in a balanced diet?

Its fiber and water content aid satiety and digestion while antioxidants help reduce oxidative stress when eaten as part of a varied diet.

Storage & handling

Store whole fruit at room temperature for a few days or refrigerate for up to a week. Once cut, keep in an airtight container and consume within 2–3 days.

What dragon fruit is

Dragon fruit, also called pitaya or pitahaya, comes from several cactus species. It has leathery, scale-tipped skin and speckled flesh that can be white, pink, or deep red with tiny edible black seeds.

Native to Central and South America, dragon fruit is now widely grown across Southeast Asia and beyond. The fruit‚Äôs flavor ranges from mildly sweet to tangy depending on the variety, and its texture is similar to kiwi due to the edible seeds.

Nutritional value

Dragon fruit is low in calories and high in water, fiber, and vitamin C. It contains minerals such as magnesium and antioxidant pigments like betalains and flavonoids.

Life cycle

  1. Establishment: Grown from seed or cuttings and trained on trellises.
  2. Vegetative growth: Stems expand and branch, positioned for sunlight.
  3. Flowering: Large night-blooming flowers open for pollination.
  4. Fruit maturation: Fruit ripens in ~40 days after pollination.
  5. Harvest: Picked when skin and firmness indicate ripeness.

Fun facts

  • Dragon fruit flowers bloom at night and attract bats and moths.
  • The fruit is about 90% water, making it hydrating and refreshing.
  • Seeds contain omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids.
  • Red flesh varieties are rich in betalain pigments.

Nutritional Benefits

  • Rich in antioxidants like vitamin C and betalains
  • High in fiber for digestive health
  • Low in calories and naturally hydrating
  • Contains magnesium, iron, and healthy fats from seeds

Simple Recipes

  • Smoothie Bowl: Blend dragon fruit, banana, mango, and almond milk. Top with granola and chia seeds.
  • Fruit Salad: Mix chopped dragon fruit, kiwi, watermelon, and mint. Drizzle with lime juice.
  • Popsicles: Puree dragon fruit with coconut water and freeze in molds.
  • Parfait: Layer dragon fruit with Greek yogurt and granola.

Fun Facts

  • Dragon fruit flowers bloom only at night and last just one evening.
  • Pollinated by bats and moths in the wild.
  • Comes in white, red, and yellow-fleshed varieties.
  • The cactus plant can live up to 20 years.
  • Seeds resemble kiwi seeds and are edible.
  • Red-fleshed dragon fruit is especially high in antioxidants.
  • Dragon fruit is native to Central America but widely grown in Asia.
